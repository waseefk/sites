The Workshop This Week 2

14th August 201420th October 2016 by Lee

We only use industry-leading fixtures and fittings for your kitchen. Beautifully crafted to provide that extra touch of quality, the neat design results in more usable space, being more pleasing to use and easier on the eye.

Here’s one of our joiners working on a solid Oak faceframe. Although designed and manufactured using state-of-the-art automated processes, we assemble our cabinets by hand as nothing can beat the finishing skills of an expert joiner.

One of our stripped back ‘Component’ cabinets; the finest precision-cut materials ready to be assembled, painted & fitted by you. This offers you total freedom with your kitchen since all we do is provide the components and deliver it to your door.

This in-frame designed cabinet has been requested with maple frames, doors and drawer fronts. Maple’s intricate lines gently glow in the sunlight, lightening up darker kitchens. Maple is an incredibly dense timber too, 30 per cent harder than Oak.

A few cabinet doors drying in our spray booth. We use specialist two part paints on our cabinets to produce a stunning and smooth finish that is more durable than traditional and water based paints.

Wanting something a little bit more distinctive, this customer has asked for their cabinets to be in luxurious Walnut.
